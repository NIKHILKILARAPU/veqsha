About us

The Agriculture Department of Andhra Pradesh, post-bifurcation in 2014, has evolved into a dynamic, farmer-centric institution working towards productivity, profitability, and sustainability in agriculture. Through strategic programs, technology integration, and farmer empowerment, it continues to serve the needs of the agrarian community. Agriculture Department of the newly formed State of Andhra Pradesh was restructured to cater to the specific agro-climatic, economic, and administrative needs of the residual state

Vision

Our vision is to make agriculture profitable profession and to improve the living standards of the farmers of Andhra Pradesh in a sustainable manner

Mission

To provide services which can help farmers to transform subsistence agriculture to commercial agriculture without disturbing ecological balance. To record higher productivity to withstand international competition by promoting diversification of cultivation of crops

Purpose

To achieve an increased GVA growth by adopting changes in cropping patterns and adoption of latest agricultural practices for increasing the crop production and productivity through effective technology transfer from Lab to Land and its acceptability by farmers. To reduce production costs through biological methods, increase farmers' agricultural income, and empower farmers to sustainably use land and water resources

Revenune generated

Andhra Pradesh's farming generates substantial revenue, with horticulture alone bringing around ₹1.55 lakh crore from 18 lakh hectares (as of May 2025), while traditional agriculture adds another ₹55,000 crore, highlighting the sector's economic importance, with government goals focusing on boosting high-income horticultural crops and integrating modern techniques like drip irrigation and solar power to double farmer incomes.
